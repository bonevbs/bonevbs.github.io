Boris Bonev is a Senior Research Scientist at NVIDIA Research in Zurich. His research lies at the intersection of numerical methods and machine learning for science and engineering, with emphasis on reliable AI for weather and climate prediction.

He completed a [Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics]({{ '/files/thesis_compressed.pdf' | relative_url }}) at the Swiss Federal Institute of Technology (EPFL) with Jan S. Hesthaven, after degrees in Physics and Aerospace Engineering from the University of Stuttgart. At NVIDIA, he worked under Anima Anandkumar and Alex Keller, and collaborated closely with Thorsten Kurth, Michael Pritchard, and William Collins. His recent work includes [Spherical Fourier Neural Operators](https://developer.nvidia.com/blog/modeling-earths-atmosphere-with-spherical-fourier-neural-operators/) and contributions to the [FourCastNet](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.12144) family of models; earlier work spans hierarchical-matrix preconditioners for wave problems and discontinuous Galerkin methods for global tsunami modeling.

Further detail is on the [research]({{ '/research/' | relative_url }}) and [software]({{ '/software/' | relative_url }}) pages.
