Welcome! I am a Senior Research Scientist at NVIDIA Research in Zurich. My background is in classical numerical methods—**numerical linear algebra** and **numerical solvers for PDEs**—and I work on the reliable use of machine learning in science and engineering. My research spans **ML methods for science and engineering** (such as AI weather and climate models), the **numerics of ML methods**, **geometric machine learning**, and **uncertainty quantification**.

I pioneered [Spherical Fourier Neural Operators](https://developer.nvidia.com/blog/modeling-earths-atmosphere-with-spherical-fourier-neural-operators/) and contribute to the [FourCastNet](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.12144) family of models, with research adopted at operational scale (NOAA, ECMWF, and others). Earlier work includes hierarchical-matrix preconditioners for wave problems and discontinuous Galerkin methods for global tsunami modeling.

I earned my [Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics]({{ '/files/thesis_compressed.pdf' | relative_url }}) from [EPFL](https://www.epfl.ch) under Prof. Jan S. Hesthaven, and hold degrees in Physics and Aerospace Engineering from the University of Stuttgart. More detail is on the [research]({{ '/research/' | relative_url }}) and [software]({{ '/software/' | relative_url }}) pages.
